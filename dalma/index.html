Dr Dalma Demeter

Arbitration. Commercial Law.

About Dalma

With ten years’ experience in teaching a range of law subjects, both online and face-to-face, Dalma’s strength lies in advanced professional training. She has a passion for coaching arbitration advocacy skills, reflected in the success of her teams at the Vis and Vis East Arbitration Moots in Vienna and Hong Kong.

Combining teaching with practice, Dalma is also an independent arbitrator and consultant in international commercial disputes. Her primary expertise is in the arbitration procedure, with a deep understanding of international sales, transport law, and contract interpretation. Dalma’s background in both civil and common law provides her clients with specialized arbitration expertise.

She advocates for global legal development and harmonization and serves as Deputy Chair of the UNCITRAL National Coordination Committee for Australia (UNCCA). Dalma is also a member of several professional organizations globally.

Qualifications

Dalma earned her LLB from Dimitrie Cantemir Christian University, Romania, in 1997, and passed the state exam at Babes-Bolyai University. She completed an LLM in International Business Law at Central European University (CEU), Budapest, in 2004, with her thesis on Ad Hoc vs. Institutional Arbitration under the supervision of Prof. Tibor Varady.

Between 2006 and 2011, Dalma pursued a professional doctorate in International Business Law at CEU, focusing on international commercial arbitration. She taught at the University of Canberra for eight years, until 2019, and has since taught at Bond and Macquarie Universities. In 2022, she published Arbitration in Australia with Oxford University Press. In 2024, she became a solicitor in Australia.

Research

Dalma’s research focuses mainly on dispute resolution and legal education. She regularly presents at conferences and publishes peer-reviewed journal articles and book chapters.

Her sabbatical research in 2016 included visits to the Max Planck Institute and UNCITRAL in Vienna. Dalma was also a member of the Executive of the Academic Review Committee for the Global Pound Conference Series on Shaping the Future of Dispute Resolution and contributed to one of the largest empirical projects in the field.

Legal Practice

Dalma specializes in international arbitration, international sales, contracts, dispute resolution, and international business law. Fluent in English, Hungarian, and Romanian, she has expertise in both civil and common law systems. Since moving to Australia in 2010, she has been appointed as a sole arbitrator in domestic cases and is available for international arbitration appointments.

Teaching

In principle, I teach law. In reality, I determine students to learn and develop into fully grown professionals ready to face the challenges of the legal profession.

Dalma Demeter, 2023

Since starting as a teaching assistant during her doctoral studies in 2006, Dalma has been passionate about teaching. In 2010, she accepted a full-time academic position at the University of Canberra, where she developed and taught courses in arbitration, sales law, and mooting, and contributed to curriculum development.

In 2016, Dalma became Course Leader for a fully online LLM in International Commercial Law and Dispute Resolution, initially with the Swiss International Law School and later with Bond University. She continues to teach international dispute resolution and sales law in this practice-oriented program.

Dalma’s teaching has earned her four awards and recognition for coaching teams to victory in the 2013 International Commercial Arbitration Moot in Hong Kong.
